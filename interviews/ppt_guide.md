# 🏢 Pre-Placement Talk (PPT) Guide
### GreyOrange Software Support Intern | June 23-24, 2026 Drive

The Pre-Placement Talk (PPT) is the very first event on Day 1 of the hiring drive. Most students treat the PPT as a passive session, but recruiters use this time to observe who is engaged, who is taking notes, and who asks structured, high-value questions. 

---

## 🎯 Why the PPT Matters!
1. **Recruiter Observation**: Panel members sit at the back or sides and look for candidates showing active interest.
2. **Cheat Code for GD & Interviews**: Facts dropped during the PPT (e.g., specific clients, new warehouse sites, software releases) can be woven into your Group Discussion and interviews.
3. **The Q&A Hook**: Asking a smart, technically sound question at the end of the PPT is the easiest way to get your face and name remembered by the hiring panel before the elimination rounds begin.

---

## 💬 Smart Questions to Ask at the End of the PPT
When the presenter asks, *"Are there any questions?"*, raise your hand immediately. Stand up, state your name and university branch clearly, and ask one of these structured questions:

### Question 1: Focused on Distributed Systems (Highly Recommended)
> *"Good morning, sir. I'm Ravi from Lovely Professional University. I read about the GreyMatter platform's Multiagent Orchestration. From a Software Support perspective, what are the most common networking or synchronization bottlenecks you encounter when coordinating hundreds of Ranger AMRs in a single warehouse, and how are these diagnosed in real time?"*
* **Why it works**: Shows you understand that robots are part of a distributed system and that network synchronization is a critical support challenge.

### Question 2: Focused on Support Operations & Global SLAs
> *"Thank you for the presentation. As a Software Support Engineer supporting global operations 24x7, how does GreyOrange manage shift handovers for critical, unresolved P1 incidents to ensure that MTTR (Mean Time to Resolve) remains within the committed SLA when transferring tickets across global support hubs?"*
* **Why it works**: Drops terminology like "shift handovers", "global operations", "MTTR", and "SLA", showing you understand how international enterprise support operates.

### Question 3: Focused on gStore & Database Sync
> *"Sir, you mentioned that the gStore platform utilizes RFID to achieve 99% inventory accuracy in retail stores. What are the common database replication or sensor sync challenges that support engineers troubleshoot when integrating gStore with a client's legacy WMS (Warehouse Management System)?"*
* **Why it works**: References a specific product (gStore) and lists standard integration challenges (database replication, legacy WMS), showing prep.

### Question 4: Focused on Success Metrics in the Support Role
> *"For a Software Support Intern, how is performance measured during the one-year internship? Is it evaluated based on SLA adherence and ticket closure volumes, or is there an emphasis on developing automation scripts to prevent recurring incidents?"*
* **Why it works**: Shows you are career-minded, proactive, and want to add value through automation scripting (which is a preferred qualification for the role).

## ⚠️ What to Avoid During the PPT
* ❌ **Using Your Phone**: Keep your phone in your pocket. Do not check notifications or chat during the presentation.
* ❌ **Fidgeting or Dozing Off**: Sit in the front-middle rows if possible. Maintain an upright posture and nod occasionally.
* ❌ **Asking Trivial Questions**: Never ask questions about salary, stipends, location, or shift timings during the public Q&A. These details are already in the job sheet and asking them shows lack of research.
* ❌ **Not Writing Anything Down**: Bring a clean notebook and a pen. Write down key points, presenter names, and facts.
